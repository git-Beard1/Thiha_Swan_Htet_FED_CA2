| What is Sustainability?

With the advance in technology, the world is becoming more and more modernised. On the other hand, it can also be said that our world is becoming more and more polluted too. It is our earth that makes our lives beautiful, better and blissful. Just like how our parents take care of us, we should also take care of our planet. Fortunately, there are many ways to be sustainable.

Public Transportation

Transportation is one of the main contributors to the emission of carbon dioxide. According to the statics, 97 percent of the global warming potential of all greenhouse gas emissions are from transportation.

Growing Trees

The abdundance of greenhouse gases leads to the increase of global temperature. However, trees can help address this issue. This is done by absorbing C02 in the atmosphere and storing it in its wood.

Recycle

Reusable items should be discarded at the assigned recycling bins. If not, the landfills will be filled up and pollute the environment. As they are eliminated through inceneration, toxic gases are also released.

Energy Conservation

Fossils fuels, such as coal, oil, and natural gas, are the main sources of energy. These resources are used up everyday for our needs. As they are unrenewable resources, they will surely run out one day.

Plastic Free

Plastics are non-biodegradable materials. Therefore, it will take hundreds of years for plastic to decompose. Furthermore, poisonous gases are released into the atmosphere upon burning.

Zero Waste

Producing goods and services require a wide range of raw materials. A lot of the resources will be depleted if not managed carefully. This will lead the future generations to suffer from the lack and shortage of resources.

| My Sustainability

I love sleep, so my mom often had to drive me school. However, ever since I learnt the carbon emissions, I decided to take MRT. Not only has it help me to become more sustainable, it also helps me build good hygienes as I have to wake up early to catch the train.
I always make sure that I turn off the lights, fans and air-conditioners. I used to open the air-conditioners all day long to keep myself comfortable. But now, I opened the windows across my study table. The feeling of cold breeze softening against cheeks is the best feeling.
I used to pack items with plastics bag. But now, I carry a reusable or crouch bag with me. Plastics are non biodegradable. However, paper bags are recyclable. They can used for composting, meaning they can convert organic materials to rich-soil nutrition when discarded.

The UNSDGs

The UNSDGs are a collection of 17 interlinked goals designed to be a "blueprint to achieve a better and more sustainable future for all. The 1 7 goals were adopted to tackle global challenges such as health improvement, poverty, inequality, climate change, environmental degradation, peace and justice.

UNICEF

UNICEF was established in the aftermath of World War II to help children who are victims of that conflict. It reaches out to whichever child in need, be it in times of war or peace. What they work for is noble and they have been doing it for more than 70 years. Nothing is more important than the well-being of children.

National Geographic

National Geographic is a non-profit organization that uses the power of science, exploration, education and storytelling to illuminate and protect the wonder of our world. Through articles and mesmerizing photography, they raise awareness for the planet's most pressing challenges.

World Health Organisation

The World Health Organization (WHO) is reknowned for its work in the field of public health. It is a specialized organisation where the member states agree to work together to respond to health emergencies and to achieve the highest attainable level of health for all people.

World Resources Institute

WRI is a global research organization that spans more than 60 countries. The institute focuses by using research-based approaches to tackle people's essential needs. They work with governments, businesses, and civil society to build transformative solutions that protect the earth and improve people's lives.

World Wildlife Fund

Founded in Singapore, WWF encourages individuals, communities and businesses to make a positive change in their lifestyle to help conserve nature and the environment. They work with governments, and other non-governmental organizations to protect the biodiversity.
